Privacy and data policy
FCDO PairUp — internal staff tool
How your data is stored

All data you enter into PairUp is stored exclusively on FCDO internal systems hosted within the UK on Microsoft Azure infrastructure. No data is transferred to, stored on, or processed by any external third-party service or platform.

What this tool is for

PairUp is solely for helping FCDO staff find compatible job share partners. Your profile data — name, grade, role preferences, working days, style, and location — is used only for this matching purpose. It will not be used for any other purpose.

Who can see your data

Your profile is visible only to other staff who are also registered on PairUp, and only within the app. No information is shared with HR, line managers, or any other part of the organisation. Connection details are only exchanged when both parties have mutually expressed interest. The tool administrators can see aggregate statistics (e.g. number of users) but not individual profile contents.

Your rights and controls

You can update or delete your profile at any time using the “Delete my profile” option. Deletion permanently removes all your data from the system. You are under no obligation to participate, and opting out has no bearing on your employment, performance, or any HR process.

GDPR compliance

PairUp is operated under the lawful basis of legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR) — specifically, supporting staff mobility and flexible working. The data collected is limited to what is strictly necessary for matching (data minimisation), is not retained beyond active participation, and is held securely. A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) has been completed for this tool. If you have questions about how your data is handled, contact the FCDO Data Protection Officer.

Ethical commitments

Participation in PairUp is entirely voluntary. The matching algorithm considers only work-related preferences you explicitly provide; it does not infer or use any protected characteristics. No automated decisions are made about your employment — the tool only suggests potential partners for you to consider. All matching is advisory and the final decision rests entirely with you.

Questions? Contact flexible.working@fcdo.gov.uk or the FCDO Data Protection Officer.
About FCDO PairUp
How the app works and how matches are scored
What is PairUp?

PairUp helps FCDO staff find compatible job share partners during the restructure. Finding a job share partner who wants the same role, works complementary days, and has a compatible working style has historically taken months of informal networking. PairUp makes that search systematic, private, and quick.

You create a profile with your preferences, the app finds potential matches from other registered staff, and you can signal interest. Contact only happens when both sides opt in.

How matching works

First, PairUp applies two gates — your profile only shows people who:

  • Are the grade you are looking for (unless you relax this in search settings)
  • Share at least one directorate with you (unless you relax this)

Within those results, profiles are ranked by:

  • Day pattern complementarity — how well your working days fit together
  • How recently the person was active — fresher profiles rank higher
  • How many directorates you share beyond the minimum
  • Whether you are in the same location
  • Whether their pattern is negotiable

You control this in “How I want to find matches” on the Matches tab.

What happens when you connect

Clicking “Request” sends a signal to the other person. If they also express interest, you both see a notification. From there, you reach out directly — PairUp does not send messages on your behalf. The connection is purely a signal that both of you are open to a conversation.

Tips for getting good matches

Select a wide range of roles and directorates you’d genuinely consider — the matching works best when preferences aren’t too narrow. Be realistic about your working days. Location matters less than grade and role fit, but it’s worth knowing before you reach out.
